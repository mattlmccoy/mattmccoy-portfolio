PhD Research

Radio Frequency Additive Manufacturing

Georgia Tech • Digital Design & Manufacturing Lab

RFAM is a novel volumetric approach combining layerwise dopant deposition with volumetric RF heating to sinter polymers. Unlike laser sintering that scans sequentially, RFAM heats the entire build volume simultaneously—enabling order-of-magnitude improvements in speed, energy efficiency, and material utilization.

$150K Custom platform built from scratch
10x Potential speed improvement
13.56 MHz RF heating frequency
Custom Binder Jet Platform
RF Heating Station
Sintered Test Specimens

Research Project

Low-Cost Metrology Pipeline

Scanner-Based Diagnostics for Binder Jet AM

A scanner-based diagnostics system that quantifies droplet placement error and dopant distribution in binder jet additive manufacturing. This work replaces expensive metrology equipment with an accessible, software-driven solution.

~$100 Total system cost
$50-100K Systems it replaces

Approach

  • Standard flatbed scanner captures high-resolution layer images
  • Python image processing pipeline extracts droplet positions
  • Statistical analysis quantifies placement error and distribution uniformity
  • Results correlate with final part quality metrics
