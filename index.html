Research

Graduate Research

PhD Candidate at Georgia Tech in the Digital Design and Manufacturing Lab, advised by Dr. Carolyn Seepersad and Dr. Chris Saldaña. My research focuses on developing novel volumetric additive manufacturing processes with real, tangible outputs.

Radio Frequency Additive Manufacturing (RFAM)

A novel volumetric approach combining layerwise dopant deposition with volumetric RF heating to sinter polymers. Unlike laser sintering that scans sequentially, RFAM heats the entire build volume simultaneously, enabling order-of-magnitude improvements in speed, energy efficiency, and material utilization.

What I Built

  • Commercial-scale custom binder jet printer with precision motion and jetting
  • 13.56 MHz RF heating station with impedance matching network
  • Complete mechanical fixturing, powder handling, and thermal instrumentation
  • Data acquisition, monitoring, and automation software
  • Secured ~$150K internal funding for equipment and development

Low-Cost Metrology Pipeline

Scanner-based diagnostics system that quantifies droplet placement error and dopant distribution in binder jet additive manufacturing. Replaces $50-100K metrology systems with a ~$100 flatbed scanner and Python software pipeline.
